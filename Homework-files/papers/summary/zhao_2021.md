# Zhao et al., 2021 — Obstacle Avoidance of Multi-Sensor Intelligent Robot Based on Road Sign Detection

**Citation / Goal**
- Zhao et al., 2021. "Obstacle Avoidance of Multi-Sensor Intelligent Robot Based on Road Sign Detection" (Sensors, 2021).
- Goal: show that ultrasonic-based avoidance is improved when fused with higher-level scene/task cues (camera/IR); produce task-aware local control.

**Sensors / Context**
- Ultrasonic range sensors + camera (road-sign detection) + infrared sensors for pit detection. Ground-robot experiments.

**Core idea**
- Ultrasonic-only avoidance tends to be myopic or follow fixed avoidance routes. Combining ultrasonic cues with camera/IR scene understanding enables task-aware avoidance that respects both local geometry and high-level mission cues.

**Design implications for RL**
- Always include `target vector` or other task context in the observation so sonar cues are interpreted relative to the mission objective (avoid obstacles while still moving toward the target).
- Don't treat sonar as a single binary "too close" event; feed normalized ranges and risk features into the policy along with the target direction so learned behavior is goal-directed.

**Implementation notes**
- Include the target-vector (dx, dy, dz scaled) in the observation and balance reward shaping so progress-to-goal remains meaningful relative to risk penalties.

**Report language snippets**
- "Zhao et al. support interpreting ultrasonic proximity cues together with task context. We provide the PPO policy with a normalized target vector so avoidance decisions remain mission-aligned rather than purely reactive." 

---

*Notes for implementers:* Include target vector features early in the observation vector and check that reward shaping (progress reward) is strong enough to keep the policy task-directed.
