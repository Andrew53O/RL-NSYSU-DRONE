# Li et al., 2024 — State-Tracking Collision Detection and Improved Potential Field

**Citation / Goal**
- Li et al., 2024. "An Obstacle Avoidance Strategy for AUV Based on State-Tracking Collision Detection and Improved Artificial Potential Field." Journal of Marine Science and Engineering.
- Goal: compute proactive collision risk by tracking relative state and use the risk to guide avoidance (classical control / planning contribution).

**Sensors / Context**
- Sonar and state estimates (position, velocity). Focus is on predicting collision risk with moving obstacles in dynamic environments.

**Core idea**
- Collision risk depends on relative motion, not just instantaneous distance. Track state (relative velocity, predicted closest approach) to compute a forward-looking risk estimate.
- Use that risk in planning/control (potential-field modifications) and as a penalty or observation feature for higher-level controllers.

**Risk modeling**
- Predict relative trajectories over a short horizon and compute predicted closest distance / time-to-closest-approach.
- Map predicted closeness into a scalar risk score used for planning or reward shaping.

**Approach trend / proactive penalty**
- Penalize positive approach trends (sonar range decreasing) or high predicted risk to encourage earlier avoidance.
- Simple proxy (suitable for RL if full prediction is expensive): normalized one-step trend = prev_norm - curr_norm; include as observation and penalize large positive values.

**Implementation notes for RL**
- If computational cost is manageable, implement a lightweight predictive risk estimator using current velocity and obstacle bearing to compute a predicted closest approach distance.
- Otherwise, include trend and recent min features as a proxy; Li motivates the value of such proactive features.

**Report language snippets**
- "Li et al. motivate tracking collision risk before impact. We include approach-trend and recent-minimum features in the RL observation and penalize increasing risk to encourage proactive avoidance rather than reactive emergency maneuvers." 

---

*Notes for implementers:* Consider adding a `predicted_risk` feature if you can cheaply compute relative motion to nearby obstacles; otherwise use the simple trend proxy from the observation design.
